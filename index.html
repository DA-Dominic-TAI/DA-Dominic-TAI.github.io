Universal Chance Lost Estimation

1. Background

Historical Limitations and the Challenge

In the retail sector, maintaining optimal inventory buffers is critical, yet true real-time stock visibility remains a persistent operational challenge. This visibility gap creates a significant analytical blind spot: only sales can be tracked, but not demand. Without a robust method to estimate lost sales (i.e. chance loss) —the sales that would have occurred had stock been available—our data reflects only supply constraints rather than actual customer intent. Quantifying these missed opportunities is the prerequisite for uncovering True Demand, a metric that is essential for calibrating high-precision forecasting models and driving genuine inventory optimization.

Historically, our capability to estimate lost sales (i.e. chance loss) was restricted to "non-PA" environments—simplified ecosystems characterized by single-daily replenishment and same-day expiration logic. However, replicating this approach for PA shops or other low sales non-omusubi items presented significant mathematical complexities that legacy models could not address, necessitating a more advanced analytical framework.

2. Methodology: Hidden Markov Modeling

The Concept: "Reading Between the Lines"

To bridge the visibility gap, we utilize the Hidden Markov Model (HMM). This probabilistic framework acts as a decoder: it takes the data we can see (sales transactions) to mathematically infer the reality we cannot see (shelf availability).

The model operates by analyzing four core components:

  • The Hidden Reality (Hidden States): The true status of the shelf at any given hour—either Available (In-Stock) or Empty (Out-of-Stock). This is the "unknown" we are solving for.
  • The Visible Evidence (Observations): The actual hourly sales data recorded by the POS system. This is the only hard evidence the model has access to.
  • The Dynamics (Transition Probabilities): The mathematical likelihood of the shelf status changing over time (e.g., the probability of an item going from "Available" to "Empty" as the day progresses).
  • The Signal (Emission Probabilities): The expected sales behavior for each state. For example, if the shelf is full, we expect frequent sales signals; if the shelf is empty, the model expects "silence" (zero sales).

4. Operational Impact

This sophisticated analytics solution fundamentally enhances inventory management through two key capabilities:

Causal Demand Estimation

Through explicit modeling of the latent availability mechanism, the system recovers unobserved demand lost to stockouts, yielding accurate demand forecasts that decouple observed sales from availability constraints—a critical distinction for robust demand planning.

Intelligent Replenishment Logic

The HMM's inference of availability states unlocks sophisticated, data-centric replenishment strategies that automatically adjust to demand dynamics. Rather than relying on fixed thresholds or reactive inventory management, the model identifies optimal replenishment opportunities by distinguishing between genuine low demand and stockout-induced sales gaps.

Ordering Pipeline Automation

1. Background and Chanllenges

Legacy Process Complexity

The legacy ordering process was characterized by high operational friction and complexity. Orchestrating orders for a diverse network of shops involved navigating multifaceted constraints, including item-specific availability windows, allocation quotas, and varying shop inventories.

Operational Burden

This manual workflow was labor-intensive and error-prone, requiring approximately 5 hours daily—effectively consuming a team member's entire morning capacity on ordering operations alone.

2. Strategic Intervention: End-to-End Automated Ordering Pipeline

Solution Architecture

To dismantle these operational bottlenecks, we deployed a scalable End-to-End Automated Ordering Pipeline. Designed around a 'Configure-then-Run' paradigm, this solution shifts the user’s role from manual execution to strategic parameter setting, ensuring precision with minimal intervention.

Core Capabilities
  • Deployed on AWS Cloud to ensure high availability, scalability, and secure remote access
  • Applies complex business logic and constraint algorithms to calculate optimal order quantities
  • Executes automatically on a daily schedule, reducing human intervention to a final verification checkpoint
  • Clean and Traceable with comprehensive logging and audit trails for full end-to-end visibility

3. Operational Impact

Efficiency Transformation

The transition from manual calculation to automated orchestration has delivered immediate efficiency gains. The daily time expenditure for ordering has collapsed from 5 hours to approximately 30 minutes.

Daily Capacity Unlocked 4.5 hours per day

This efficiency gain allows the operations team to reallocate bandwidth toward high-value strategic tasks rather than repetitive administration, improving overall productivity and decision-making quality.

4. Collaborative Decision Support (Continuing Development)

Stakeholder Empowerment

The next phase of development focuses on augmenting the pipeline with a Decision Support System (DSS) interface. This front-end layer will empower non-technical stakeholders to contribute directly to the ordering strategy.

Shop Teams and PPD (Product Planning & Distribution) experts will be able to input qualitative insights directly into the model. By synthesizing algorithmic logic with human expertise, the pipeline will evolve from a purely operational tool into a strategic asset for smarter inventory management.
